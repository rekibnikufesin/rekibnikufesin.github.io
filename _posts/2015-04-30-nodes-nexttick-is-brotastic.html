It’s true. Node’s nextTick function is simply brotastic. You just can’t argue that. </p>


If you aren’t familiar with it, you may be questioning my logic. Bear with me fellow brosefs, I’ll make myself understood. Consider a piece of code like this:
console.log(‘start’);
process.nextTick(function() {
console.log(‘nextTick is brotastic’);
});
console.log(‘scheduled’);</p>

When you run it, you’ll see the console output:
</p>

When node encounters the nextTick function, it skips it for now, but will execute it on the next cycle. It’s the equivalent of asking a bro if he wants to go for pizza, to which he replies, “nah bro- but hit me up next time!"</p></a>


Remembering that node is an event loop processor, we can understand that it can do this as it just loops through the application over and over. Why not just use
setTimeout(function, 0);
then? Answer: performance. The nextTick function does not require the IO resources that setTimeout does, so while functionally they are the same- under heavy load nextTick scales much better and leaves more resources for your app.</p>

May the bro be with you.</p>
